I am an Italian researcher, artist and educator working hard to bridge the gaps in geography. I am an Assistant Professor at the Universidad de Valladolid and a Researcher Collaborator at the Núcleo Interdisciplinar de Comunicação Sonora (NICS), Universidade Estadual de Campinas (UNICAMP). I am also Associate Editor at the International Journal of Art Therapy.

My work lives at the crossroads of music, technology, and the body. I design and study Extended Digital Musical Instruments (EDMIs) — instruments that do not require physical contact, that respond to movement, colour, and gesture — and I explore how these can transform therapeutic, educational, and artistic experience. My research is grounded in embodied cognition and philosophy of mind: I believe that music is not just something we hear, but something we do, feel, and become.

Beyond the laboratory, I compose, create installations, and collaborate with dancers, therapists, neuroscientists, and engineers. I find meaning in the spaces between disciplines — where a question from music therapy becomes a design problem, and a design problem opens a philosophical one.

What is e-mocomu?

e-mocomu is an Extended Digital Musical Instrument (EDMI) that maps bodily movement, colour perception, and real-time sound generation into a single interactive experience. Developed and patented at the Universidad de Valladolid (2016), it embodies the idea that music-making can be free from physical objects — driven by the body itself.

The instrument translates motion and colour data into live musical output, enabling participants — including those with motor or cognitive difficulties — to engage in genuine musical creation through free movement in space. It has been applied in therapeutic, pedagogical, and artistic performance contexts.

e-motion

Body movement and proprioception as primary musical gesture — translating motion into expressive, real-time sound.

Colours

Real-time colour input as a musical parameter, connecting visual perception and aesthetic experience to sound.

Music

Live, generative musical output shaped by the participant's own bodily and sensory engagement with the space.

Inclusive Design

Accessible, low-cost technology — conceived for rehabilitation, creative empowerment, and live performance.
